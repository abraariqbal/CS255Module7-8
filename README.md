Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
The DriverPass project focused on designing a system for a driving company that wanted to imrpove how students prepare for their driving exams.
The client needed a web-based system that would let students to register, schedule lessons, take practice tests, and manage payments. Administrators and staff needed tools to manage package options,
monitor accounts, and keep system security. 

What did you do particularly well?
An area I think I did particularly well was creating the UML diagrams. I was able to take the client's requirements and translate them into activity diagrams, sequence diagrams, and a class diagram
that showed the system functionality. The diagrams helped show the system behavior, user interactions, and data relationships. 

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I were to revise a part of my work, I would clarify some of the relationships in my class diagram more and expand on some of the nonfunctional requirements. While the system met the clients needs,
I could improve the clarity by adding more detailed performance and scalability thoughts.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
To interpret the user's needs, I looked over the business requiremnts and translated them into functional and nonfunctional requirements. From there, I implemented those into my design by modeling use cases,
activity flows, and system interations. Keeping the customer's needs in mind is crucial because a system that doesn't meet the customer's expecations will fail regardless of how good technically the system is.

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
When I design software, I first focus on clearly identifying the system requirements. I model the system using UML diagrams before looking at implementation. This lets me identify logic gaps, security concerns,
and potential improvements early in the design phase. In the future, I plan to continue using modeling techniques, stakeholder analysis, and consistent feedback to make sure that the systems are functional and
reliable.
